Project 1 - Imove (Lake Lucerne Institute)

This project aims to assess upper limb motor deficits in post-stroke patients by analyzing their movements during a drinking task. The goal is to improve personalized rehabilitation through precise movement segmentation.

Steps:

  • ✔️ Extensive literature review on movement analysis and post-stroke motor disorders.
  • ✔️ Python development of a pipeline to segment movements into motor primitives.
  • ✔️ Creation of a Python graphical interface to facilitate manual segmentation of experimental videos.
  • ✔️ Evaluation of a metric based on this segmentation to quantify patient performance.
2025

Project 2 - Ultrasound Image Quality Assessment Based on Confidence Map (Politecnico di Milano)

Project conducted during my Erasmus at Politecnico di Milano, aiming to establish a confidence map for ultrasound images acquired by a robotic hand on a subject. The objective is to evaluate image quality and quantify information loss caused by downsampling.

Steps:

  • ✔️ Acquisition of ultrasound images from the robotic hand.
  • ✔️ Calculation of the confidence map for each image.
  • ✔️ Application of downsampling to reduce image resolution.
  • ✔️ Similarity calculation to assess the impact of downsampling on data loss.

Project 3 - Milken (INSERM)

This project consists of analyzing multichannel EEG signals recorded from ALS model mice using sensors placed on their skulls over several days. The goal is to better understand neurological alterations related to ALS by improving signal quality and developing efficient analysis tools.

Steps:

  • ✔️ Analysis of multichannel EEG signals recorded from ALS model mice via sensors placed on their skulls over several days.
  • ✔️ Preprocessing of raw EEG data to improve signal quality.
  • ✔️ Study of EEG signals to better understand neurological alterations linked to ALS.
  • ✔️ Development of Python tools to facilitate data analysis and interpretation on Matlab.

Visualization of different EEG frequency bands (delta, theta, alpha, beta) extracted from recordings on ALS model mice conducted during the project.

Project 4 - Study of Mechanical Properties of a Membrane (Icube)

A detailed analysis was carried out to understand the mechanical behavior of a membrane subjected to different stresses, aiming to characterize its physical properties and resistance.

Steps:

  • ✔️ Initial program configuration to allow processing of experimental data.
  • ✔️ Improvement of the quality of obtained images to facilitate precise deformation analysis.
  • ✔️ Calculation of volume and curvature to quantify mechanical variations of the membrane.
  • ✔️ In-depth analysis of results to interpret physical behavior and identify key characteristics.
